Fried Chicken Recipe

About Fried Chicken

This fried chicken recipe epitomizes the essence of Southern comfort food with its crispy, golden-brown exterior and tender, juicy interior. Starting with plump chicken pieces marinated in a buttermilk bath overnight, the meat absorbs a tangy richness that tenderizes and enhances flavor. Dredged in a seasoned flour mixture featuring paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper, each piece is meticulously coated, ensuring a crunchy, flavorful crust upon frying. Carefully lowered into hot oil until beautifully browned and cooked through, the chicken emerges with a satisfying crunch that gives way to moist, succulent meat. Served piping hot alongside classic sides like fluffy biscuits, creamy mashed potatoes, and tangy coleslaw, this fried chicken dish promises a soul-warming experience that embodies the essence of home-cooked comfort and Southern hospitality.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Buttermilk
  • Flour (Seasoned with salt, pepper, and paprika)
  • Egg
  • Oil
  1. Rinse and pat dry chicken pieces (drumsticks, thighs, breasts).
  2. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Dip each piece in flour seasoned with salt, pepper, and any desired spices (like paprika or garlic powder). Shake off excess flour.
  4. Heat about 1 inch of oil in a skillet or pot over medium-high heat until it reaches 350F (175C).
  5. Carefully place chicken pieces in the hot oil, skin-side down if using skin-on pieces.
  6. Fry for about 6-8 minutes per side, until golden brown and cooked through. Larger pieces may require longer cooking times.
  7. Remove chicken from oil and drain on paper towels or a wire rack.
